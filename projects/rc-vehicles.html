5 Inch Quad

  • Frame: Martian II RX250
  • Flight Controller: Mambda F405 Mk3 Stack
  • Motors: Emax ECO Series 2207s
  • FPV Camera: Foxeer Falkor
  • HD Camera: GoPro Hero 5 session
  • VTx: Eachine TX805
  • Rx: FrSky XM+
  • GPS: Beitian BN-180
  • Buzzer: ViFly Finder 2

This quad is capable of various missions at about 10 minutes of flight time with a CNHL 1500mAh 4S 120C LiPo battery.

This quad has been programmed with betaflight to return to home using it's onboard GPS, emit an independantly powered distress beep/light & has a tuned video transmitter for better video link range and quality to analogue video goggles.

3 Inch Quad

  • Frame: Armattan Gecko
  • Flight Controller: Rush FPV Mini Stack
  • Motors: Emax RS1306B V2 - 2700Kv
  • FPV Camera: Runcam Split 3 Nano
  • HD Camera: GoPro Hero 5 session
  • VTx & Rx: TBS Crossfire Sixty9
  • Buzzer: ViFly Finder 2

A new build for a new world. This quad is under 250 grams in maximum take of weight, or just 152 grams with no battery. Perfect for the new regulations for UAV's. See the adjacent wiring diagram for details on the build.

This quad is built for agility. With it's small size and powerful 2700Kv motors, it can maneuver great freestyle tricks. Whilst it's weight gives it decent flight times, even using 850 mAh 3S batteries to keep the weight down

AWD Drift Car

  • Frame: ZD Racing EX-16
  • Rx: Crossfire Nano w/ 6ch adapter

A great performing RC car with high quality components, including an aluminium chassis, AWD and front and rear lights. This car was fitted with the TBS Crossfire system, so that I could control it with a Crossfire enabled transmitter.

Micro Quad

  • FC & Vtx: betafpv Lite Brushed FC V2
  • Motors: betafpv 7x16mm 19000KV
  • FPV Camera: Caddx Ant Micro
  • Rx: Caddx Ant Micro

Assembled for indoor FPV flight, this microquad has a BETAFPV Lite Brushed Flight Controller with an interated video transmitter & pre-configured OSD.

It also works with my existing Taranis X-Lite transmitter, as I have installed an XM+ Receiver onto the flight controller, with a canopy modification to allow the antenna's to poke through like cute little deely boppers.
